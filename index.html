Strategy Playbook

Daya

How to own the China-Africa trade corridor in stablecoins
Prepared for Ayomide, Daya Date July 2026

The corridor nobody is closing

Daya already moves money from Nigerian importers to Chinese suppliers in USDC. That is one half of a corridor. The other half, what happens on the Chinese side when the supplier receives the USDC, is still broken. The supplier still needs to convert to CNY. They still deal with correspondent banks. The settlement is still slow.

If Daya closes both ends, you do not just process payments. You own the corridor. And the credit facility you already have is the wedge that makes importers choose your corridor over anyone else's.

Five partners close the loop. None of them are the obvious ones.

The partners

01

XTransfer

Closes the China side. 400,000 Chinese B2B exporters already use XTransfer for multi-currency trade settlement. They are actively exploring stablecoin rails.
The logic is this: Daya's importer sends USDC to pay a Chinese supplier. XTransfer receives the USDC on the supplier's behalf and settles in CNY to the supplier's Chinese bank account. Settlement happens in minutes instead of days. No correspondent bank. No SWIFT tracing. The supplier does not need to understand crypto. They just receive CNY in their normal bank account. XTransfer handles the off-ramp on the China side the same way Daya handles the on-ramp on the Nigeria side. Together you have a closed stablecoin corridor between Lagos and Shenzhen and nobody else in Africa has this.

02

BFREE

Closes the credit risk. BFREE manages distressed loan portfolios and has the deepest data set on informal Nigerian borrowers in the country.
The credit facility Daya already offers is the wedge, but it lives or dies on whether you can assess an importer's creditworthiness before extending a line. These importers have no bank credit history. No collateral a bank recognizes. But they do have a history of repaying informal loans, supplier credit, market association dues, mobile money overdrafts. BFREE has this data. They collect and structure it as part of their debt recovery work with Nigerian banks and fintechs. If Daya partners with BFREE, you get credit scores on importers using data nobody else has access to. The importer has never seen a bank loan officer. But BFREE already knows whether they pay their debts.

03

Moniepoint

Closes the physical cash layer. 5 million businesses, N21 trillion processed, POS agents inside the exact markets where Daya's importers trade.
Daya's importers live in cash. They receive Naira payments from their buyers in cash at Alaba, at Kariakoo, at Trade Fair. They need to convert that cash into their Daya account before they can send the next USDC payment to China. Right now the conversion step is friction. The importer takes cash, goes to a bank, deposits it, waits for clearing, moves it into Daya. A Moniepoint partnership deletes that step. The importer hands cash to a Moniepoint agent at the same market where they already trade. The agent credits their Daya balance. No bank. No clearing delay. Cash physically enters the Daya ecosystem through Moniepoint's agent network. This is an offline-to-stablecoin bridge and nobody in crypto has built it at scale in Nigeria.

04

Bumpa

Closes the repayment prediction. Bumpa has inventory and sales data on the Nigerian retailers who buy from Daya's importers.
The riskiest part of extending credit to an importer is not whether they want to pay you back. It is whether their buyers pay them. The importer gives goods to retailers on credit. The retailers sell to consumers. The retailers pay the importer. The importer repays Daya. If the retailers are slow to sell, the importer is slow to repay. Bumpa has real time inventory and sales data from thousands of Nigerian retail shops, the exact kind of business that buys from Daya's importers. If Daya integrates with Bumpa, you can see downstream sales data. When a batch of imported electronics sells fast through retailers using Bumpa, you know the importer is about to get paid. When inventory sits, you know the repayment is at risk. This is a repayment signal that no bank has.

05

Aries Markets

Closes the capital supply. Aries is the largest lending protocol on Aptos and Aptos is your investor.
The credit facility needs capital to lend. You can raise a debt round, which dilutes and takes months. Or you can tap on-chain USDC pools that already exist. Aries Markets is the largest lending protocol on Aptos with USDC lending pools. Daya is an Aptos portfolio company. A dedicated USDC credit pool on Aries, supplied by Aptos ecosystem liquidity providers who want exposure to African trade finance, is the fastest path to a working credit facility. No debt raise. No bank partnership. No six month negotiation. Just an on-chain pool with transparent terms and Daya as the borrower, using the USDC to originate credit lines for importers and repaying with spread.
